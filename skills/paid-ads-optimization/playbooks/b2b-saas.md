# Playbook: Paid Ads Optimization for B2B SaaS

B2B paid GTM starts with high-intent search and LinkedIn, not broad Meta prospecting. Hypothesis loop runs weekly; scale only when unit economics clear.

- **Day −14: Audit tracking stack.** Google: gclid → GA4 → CRM signup event. LinkedIn: Insight Tag on demo/trial thank-you page. Meta pixel + CAPI if retargeting planned. Test with Tag Assistant; one test conversion per platform.
- **Day −12: Build dedicated landing pages (2 minimum).** LP-A: Search intent — headline matches top keyword ("[Category] software for [ICP]"). LP-B: Problem-aware — "Stop [pain]" for social. Single CTA, social proof above fold, no nav links.
- **Day −10: Launch Google Search — one campaign.** Keywords: brand, "[competitor] alternative," 5 category terms. Exact + phrase match only. Budget: 40% of monthly ad spend. Ad copy: headline 1 = keyword, headline 2 = outcome, description = proof point.
- **Day −7: Launch LinkedIn — one campaign.** Objective: lead gen or website conversions. Audience: job titles + company size + industry. 3 ad variants: (1) customer quote + logo, (2) metric hook ("Cut X from 14 days to 2"), (3) founder video 30 sec. Budget: 50% of spend.
- **Day −5: Hypothesis log live.** Spreadsheet: Date, Hypothesis, Variable, Control, Variant, Primary metric, Min sample (50 conv), Status. First hypothesis: "Customer quote creative will beat feature-list creative on CPA because social proof reduces skepticism."
- **Day 0: Week 1 baseline.** Don't optimize yet. Collect 50+ clicks per ad set. Daily check: spend pacing, conversion firing, LP load speed. Kill any ad set with 0 conversions after $150 spend.
- **Days +1 to +7: First hypothesis test.** Swap lowest CTR creative for new variant (one change only). Run until 50 conversions total or 7 days. Winner → new control; loser → archive with note.
- **Days +8 to +14: Second test — LP headline.** 50/50 traffic split on LP-A: control headline vs variant (outcome-focused vs pain-focused). Measure conversion rate, not CTR.
- **Days +15 to +21: Retargeting layer (if 1k+ monthly visitors).** Meta or LinkedIn retargeting: 7-day site visitors, testimonial creative, "Still evaluating [category]? See how [Customer] did it." Budget: 10% of total. Expect 2–4× lower CPA than cold.
- **Days +22 to +30: Scale decision.** If CPA ≤ target and trial-to-paid ≥10%: increase winning campaign budget 20% every 3 days. If CPA > target: pause scale, run hypothesis on audience (narrow job titles) or offer (extended trial, demo-first CTA).

Realistic outcome: $3k–8k spend/month, $80–$180 CPA qualified trial/demo, 4–8% LP conversion, 2–3 winning creatives in rotation, 12–15% trial-to-paid from paid traffic.

## Preconditions
- [ ] Marketing profile complete for paid-ads-optimization
- [ ] Primary metric named before execution
- [ ] Honest aggression dial matches real assets (list, proof, team hours)

## Aggression dial

| Level | When | Focus |
|-------|------|-------|
| conservative | Thin assets / solo | One channel, learning metrics |
| standard | Warm channel or list | Best-practice cadence from narrative above |
| aggressive | Strong proof + distribution | Execute full tactic stack with kill rules |

## Timeline
- **T-14**: Instrument leading metric; lock ICP + offer
- **T-7**: Ship assets referenced in narrative
- **T-1**: Final checklist + supporter warm-up
- **H0**: Primary launch motion
- **D+1…D+7**: Measure, teardown, kill or scale

## Tactic stack
1. Name bottleneck with 3 profile citations
2. Ship one measurable experiment this week
3. Tie Plan Studio tasks to registry tactic ids
4. Cap parallel channels at 2
5. Predefine kill/pivot rule before spend or blast

## Orchestration
- One primary channel from narrative; ≤2 support channels
- Repo/browser/asset tasks split by execution_mode

## Realistic outcomes
- State honest bands — do not promise #1 PH or viral without assets
- Leading metric moves in 7–14d when playbook matches profile

## Kill / pivot rules
- Leading metric misses after agreed sample → stop and rewrite hypothesis
- Never add channels when primary motion underperforms

## Ethics line
- No fake metrics, fake social proof, undisclosed ads, or ToS-breaking growth hacks
