## Principles

1. Paid ads are a *hypothesis loop*, not a set-and-forget campaign. Every week: one hypothesis → one variable change → measure → kill or scale. "Creative fatigue" and "audience saturation" are hypotheses, not excuses.
2. Conversion tracking is the foundation. Pixel + CAPI (Meta), gclid + offline conversion import (Google), LinkedIn Insight Tag — all firing on qualified events (signup, trial start, demo booked), not just page views. No tracking = no optimization.
3. Start with one channel, one campaign objective, one audience. B2B SaaS: LinkedIn or Google Search for intent; Meta for retargeting only until you have 50+ conversions/month. Splitting $2k across 4 platforms learns nothing.
4. Creative is the targeting on Meta and LinkedIn. 70% of performance variance comes from ad creative, not audience settings. Plan 5–10 creative variants per month; rotate every 14–21 days before fatigue.
5. Landing page message match beats bid strategy. Ad headline and LP headline should share 3+ words. Mismatch drops conversion 40–60% regardless of CPC. Fix LP before raising budget.
6. Hypothesis format: "If we [change X], then [metric Y] will [improve/decline] because [reason]." Test one X at a time: headline, image, audience, bid cap, LP above-fold. Document in a test log.
7. Kill rules prevent budget bleed. Pre-define: pause ad set if CPA >2× target after 50 clicks or $200 spend (whichever first). Scale rule: increase budget 20% every 3 days if CPA ≤ target with 30+ conversions.
8. Retargeting is the highest-ROI layer — but only with 1,000+ monthly site visitors. Sequence: 7-day visit no convert → testimonial ad; 14-day → case study; 30-day → offer/expiry. Don't retarget cold audiences with hard sell.
9. Search ads capture intent; social ads create it. Google Search for "[category] software," "alternative to [competitor]." Social for problem-aware audiences with educational creative, not demo CTAs on cold traffic.
10. Unit economics gate scale. LTV:CAC ≥3 for SaaS before scaling spend 2×. If CPA is acceptable but trial-to-paid is 2%, you're buying vanity signups — fix funnel before budget increase.
