# LinkedIn DM — Fill-In Template

3–5 sentences max. Hand-send only — no bulk paste. **Draft for founder review.**

---

## Lead context

```
Lead:           [Name, Title @ Company]
Connection:     [1st / 2nd / cold InMail]
Trigger:        [post comment / job change / PH / mutual event]
Trigger URL:    [link]
Warmth:         [cold / warm (engaged 2+ times) / subscriber]
```

---

## Template A — Post-triggered (highest reply rate)

**When:** They commented on your post, liked 3+ posts, or published on your JTBD topic.

```
Hey [first name] — your [post/comment] on [topic] resonated. Especially [one specific detail they wrote].

We're working on [related problem] with [product] — early, but [one proof point from product profile].

Not pitching — genuinely curious how you're handling [specific sub-problem] at [company]. Worth a 10-min compare-notes chat?

— [your first name]
```

---

## Template B — ICP cold (requires 1 line real research)

**When:** Profile matches ICP, no prior engagement.

```
Hey [first name] — saw [company]'s [recent launch / hiring post / product update]. [One observation proving you looked — not generic praise].

I build [product] for [ICP role plural] dealing with [pain from JTBD]. [Named customer or design partner] saw [outcome] in [timeframe — only if verified].

If [pain] is on your radar this quarter, happy to share what's working for similar teams — 15 min, your call if relevant.

— [your first name]
```

---

## Template C — Warm subscriber / launch invite

**When:** On email list OR engaged 2+ times.

```
Hey [first name] — opening [product] to a small batch [this week/next month]. Short list because [specific reason: engaged on X / beta feedback / mutual intro from Y].

[One-line outcome from positioning statement]. Founding access: [trial terms — honest, no fake scarcity].

Interested? I'll send the link — no pressure if timing's off.

— [your first name]
```

---

## Template D — Follow-up (once, 5–7 days after no reply)

```
Hey [first name] — bumping in case this got buried. Totally fine if not a fit or bad timing.

If useful later, I'm here. Either way, appreciated your [specific post/article] on [topic].

— [your first name]
```

**Stop after Template D** unless new trigger (they posted again, intro, funding news).

## Reply handlers (draft these too when lead is hot)

**They say yes:**
```
Awesome — throw me 2 times that work or grab 15 min here: [Calendly — no UTM needed on DM]

Looking forward to it.
```

**They say "send info":**
```
90-sec walkthrough: [Loom URL ?utm_source=linkedin&utm_medium=dm]

One-pager if you want to share internally: [link]

Happy to jump on a call if anything sparks questions.
```

## Example (filled)

```
Hey Sarah — your thread on cutting ramp time from 14 to 5 days hit home. The Notion playbook duplication problem is exactly what we hear from Series B ops leads.

We built RampKit for that workflow — design partner FinOps Co got managers down to ~4 hrs/week on ramp admin in month one.

Curious if you're still iterating on that this quarter — happy to swap notes, 10 min, no pitch deck.

— Alex
```

---

## Bad vs good

| Bad | Good |
|-----|------|
| "I hope you're well" | Opens with their content |
| Pitch deck link in message 1 | Link after reply or in "send info" |
| Same DM to 20 people/day | Each DM has unique trigger line |
| "I noticed you're hiring" (false) | Verifiable hiring post URL in notes |
| 8+ sentences | 3–5 sentences |

---

## Reject the DM if:

- No specific trigger reference
- Product name in first sentence (cold outreach)
- Claims not in product profile
- Calendar link + deck link in message 1
- Third message without new trigger

## Ethical notes

- LinkedIn ToS limits automation — hand-send
- No email scraping from profile for dual-channel spam same day
- Stop after 2 unanswered messages
