# Playbook: Outreach Drafting for B2B SaaS

B2B buyers ignore vendor spam and respond to peer operators who understand their stack and buying process. Draft like a founder, not an SDR team.

- **Open with their business problem, not your fundraise.** Reference hiring post, earnings call theme, product launch, or eng blog — something that shows you understand their world.
- **One metric or named workflow in touch 1.** "Cut onboarding from 14 days to 2" or "automates the SOC 2 evidence collection step your security team hates." Vague "save time" fails with VP+ readers.
- **Match CTA to deal size signal.** Self-serve product + SMB lead → "Start a free trial — takes 10 min." Mid-market → "15-min walkthrough, no deck." Enterprise signals (SSO page, compliance) → "Happy to share our security pack + intro call."
- **LinkedIn DM default for net-new; email when you have verified work address.** InMail for unreachable profiles. Never dual-channel same day — feels like spam.
- **Touch 2: customer proof or competitive wedge.** "Team similar to yours ([industry/stage]) replaced [incumbent] for [one reason]." Only use real customers or clearly labeled "design partner."
- **Touch 3: internal-forwarding asset.** Offer a one-pager or 90-sec Loom the champion can send to their boss. B2B deals stall on internal sell — draft should help them sell upward.
- **Avoid calendar links in touch 1.** B2B buyers want to assess relevance before committing time. Calendar in touch 2 after any positive signal, or when they reply "send more info."
- **Subject line formulas that work:** "Question re: [their initiative]", "[Mutual connection] suggested I reach out" (only if true), "[Competitor] alternative for [team name]."
- **Compliance note in every pack.** CAN-SPAM/GDPR: include physical address in email footer, honor opt-out within 10 days, no false urgency ("offer expires Friday" when it doesn't).

Track: reply rate (benchmark 8–15% on A-tier cold), positive reply rate, meeting booked rate from positive replies.
