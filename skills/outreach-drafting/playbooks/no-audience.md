# Playbook: Outreach Drafting With No Audience

Cold outbound with zero brand recognition. Every message must earn attention through **specific relevance**, not volume. Draft for founder review — never send.

- **One lead, one draft, one hook.** Pull the lead's why-now from research pack. First line references their public signal (post, launch, job change) — not your product.
- **Lead with the problem they already stated.** Quote or paraphrase their words from LinkedIn post, PH comment, or company blog. "You mentioned [X] takes 3 days" beats "I noticed you're scaling."
- **Proof point from product profile only.** One sentence: what the product does + one evidence-backed outcome. No fabricated metrics, mutual connections, or "I saw you at [event]."
- **CTA = low friction, peer tone.** "Worth a 15-min compare-notes call?" or "Happy to share how [similar company type] handled this — no deck." Never "Would love 30 minutes to walk you through our platform."
- **Three-touch sequence, 3–5 business days apart.** Touch 1: problem + relevance. Touch 2: new proof (case study, screenshot described, specific workflow). Touch 3: graceful close + leave door open. No "just bumping this."
- **Channel pick by lead source.** LinkedIn-sourced lead → LinkedIn DM first. Email found on contact page → cold email. PH maker → Twitter DM or PH comment, not corporate email.
- **Subject lines for email: outcome or their company name.** "[Company] + [pain noun]" or "Re: [their post title truncated]." Never "Quick question" or "Partnership opportunity."
- **Cap batch at 10 drafts per session.** Founder should personally review and lightly edit each. Identical structure across 20 messages = spam pattern.
- **Include opt-out mentally.** Touch 3 explicitly: "Totally fine if timing's off — I'll stop here unless something changes on your end."

Track: drafts_with_evidence_anchor (target 100%), follow-ups_with_new_value (target 100%, never pure bumps), founder edit rate (high edit rate = drafts too generic).
