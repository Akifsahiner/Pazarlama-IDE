# Playbook: Lead Research With No Audience (Manual Seed List)

No outbound infrastructure, no warm network, no PH traction yet. Build a **manual seed list of 20** perfect-fit accounts from public sources — depth beats volume.

- **Define must-have criteria before opening LinkedIn.** Pull ICP from product profile: role, company stage, industry, stack, trigger event. Write 5 must-haves and 3 nice-to-haves. No criteria = stop and run `product-intelligence` first.
- **Start with 10 accounts you can name from memory.** Founders always know 10 companies that *should* use the product — ex-colleagues' employers, competitors' customers, companies discussed in niche Slack/Discord. These are Tier A seeds.
- **Expand via "customers of competitors" search.** Google: `"[competitor name]" case study`, `"[competitor]" customer`, G2 reviews. Each named customer = a lead with proven budget for the category.
- **Use community directories, not purchased lists.** Indie Hackers products page, relevant subreddit flairs, GitHub orgs using complementary OSS, niche Slack workspace member lists (with permission). Every lead needs a public source URL.
- **Find the person, not just the company.** For each account: identify one champion (role from ICP) via company About page, team page, or LinkedIn (public profile only). No email guessing — note LinkedIn URL or contact form path.
- **Document why-now per account.** Hiring for relevant role, recent funding, product launch, stack migration, regulatory change. Fit without timing = Tier B at best.
- **Score with the lead scorecard template.** Score fit dimensions 1–5. A tier = all must-haves + why-now + score ≥18/25. C tier = exclude from default outreach pack.
- **Cap at 20, deliver with source matrix.** Dedupe by domain. Track source (manual seed, competitor customer, community) to prevent double-counting if user expands later.
- **Hand off to founder for relationship check.** Flag any account where founder has a warm path (investor portco, conference meetup, mutual connection). Warm > cold even at seed stage.

Track: icp_fit_rate (target ≥60% A-tier in final 20), source diversity (≥3 source types), average why-now evidence per A-tier lead.

## Preconditions
- [ ] Marketing profile complete for lead-research
- [ ] Primary metric named before execution
- [ ] Honest aggression dial matches real assets (list, proof, team hours)

## Aggression dial

| Level | When | Focus |
|-------|------|-------|
| conservative | Thin assets / solo | One channel, learning metrics |
| standard | Warm channel or list | Best-practice cadence from narrative above |
| aggressive | Strong proof + distribution | Execute full tactic stack with kill rules |

## Timeline
- **T-14**: Instrument leading metric; lock ICP + offer
- **T-7**: Ship assets referenced in narrative
- **T-1**: Final checklist + supporter warm-up
- **H0**: Primary launch motion
- **D+1…D+7**: Measure, teardown, kill or scale

## Tactic stack
1. Name bottleneck with 3 profile citations
2. Ship one measurable experiment this week
3. Tie Plan Studio tasks to registry tactic ids
4. Cap parallel channels at 2
5. Predefine kill/pivot rule before spend or blast

## Orchestration
- One primary channel from narrative; ≤2 support channels
- Repo/browser/asset tasks split by execution_mode

## Realistic outcomes
- State honest bands — do not promise #1 PH or viral without assets
- Leading metric moves in 7–14d when playbook matches profile

## Kill / pivot rules
- Leading metric misses after agreed sample → stop and rewrite hypothesis
- Never add channels when primary motion underperforms

## Ethics line
- No fake metrics, fake social proof, undisclosed ads, or ToS-breaking growth hacks
