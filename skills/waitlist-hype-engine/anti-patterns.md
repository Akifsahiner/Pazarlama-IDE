# Anti-Patterns

- **Don't:** Launch a bare "Enter your email" form with no referral loop. **Why:** You collect emails but build zero distribution. Every signup should generate at least one share attempt.
- **Don't:** Offer cash or gift cards as referral rewards. **Why:** Attracts arbitrageurs and low-quality signups. Status rewards (queue skip, founding member badge) filter for genuine interest.
- **Don't:** Send weekly "We're working hard" updates with no new information. **Why:** Waitlist fatigue sets in by email 3. Each touch must deliver value, proof, or urgency.
- **Don't:** Gate the entire product behind a waitlist after public beta. **Why:** Kills word-of-mouth from users who can't invite peers. Waitlist works pre-launch; post-launch use invite-only cohorts instead.
- **Don't:** Fake the signup counter or social proof ticker. **Why:** Founders get caught; trust damage is permanent. Use real data or hide the counter until you have 100+ signups.
- **Don't:** Run paid ads to a waitlist with K-factor below 0.2. **Why:** You're buying emails, not a growth loop. Fix the referral mechanic first, then amplify with ads.
- **Don't:** Open the waitlist and launch on the same day. **Why:** You lose 2–4 weeks of referral compounding. Minimum 21-day runway between waitlist open and product access.

- **Don't:** Ship generic advice untied to this product profile. **Why:** Founders already have ChatGPT. **Cost:** Trust and retention.

- **Don't:** Recommend five channels at once. **Why:** Dilutes learning. **Cost:** No clear kill signal.

- **Don't:** Invent metrics or connector data. **Why:** Honest measurement is a product promise. **Cost:** Credibility collapse.

- **Don't:** Skip acceptance criteria on tasks. **Why:** Ambiguous done. **Cost:** Endless 'almost shipped'.

- **Don't:** Hide tradeoffs. **Why:** Senior operators state ceilings. **Cost:** Overpromise churn.
