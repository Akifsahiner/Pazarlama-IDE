## Principles

1. A waitlist is a *distribution engine*, not a signup form. Every element — headline, referral mechanic, confirmation email, share CTA — must answer: "Why should I tell someone else?"
2. Referral loops work when the reward is *status*, not just discounts. "Skip 500 people in line" beats "Get 10% off." Queue position is the currency of pre-launch hype.
3. The referral unit must be one click. Pre-filled tweet, WhatsApp deep link, or copy-paste invite with a unique code. Friction above one tap kills viral coefficient.
4. Hype compounds in waves, not spikes. Schedule three micro-launches before the main launch: teaser page, beta cohort invite, public waitlist open. Each wave re-activates dormant signups.
5. Social proof on the waitlist page must be *specific*. "2,847 teams on the list" beats "Join thousands." Live counter + recent signup ticker (first name + company) increases conversion 15–30%.
6. The confirmation page is your highest-intent traffic. Don't dead-end with "Thanks." Show referral rank, share assets (OG image, one-liner, 15-second clip), and a "move up" CTA immediately.
7. Segment waitlisters by referral count and engagement. Top 10% referrers get early access, a founder DM, or a named spot on the "founding members" page. Reward the amplifiers, not the lurkers.
8. Email the waitlist on a rhythm: weekly value (not "we're still building"), milestone updates ("We hit 5,000 — you're #847"), and one scarcity trigger 48 hours before launch ("Doors close Friday").
9. Cross-pollinate with launch channels. PH upcoming page, Indie Hackers build log, and founder LinkedIn posts should all funnel to the same waitlist URL with UTM-tagged referral defaults.
10. Measure viral coefficient (K-factor) weekly. If K < 0.3 after 14 days, the referral mechanic or reward is broken — fix before spending on paid traffic to the waitlist.
