## Principles

1. The hero must answer "what is it, who is it for, why now" in under 5 seconds. If a stranger can't repeat your value prop after one read, the page is broken.
2. Specificity outsells cleverness. "Cut payroll runtime from 4 hours to 9 minutes" beats "Payroll, reimagined." Numbers, named outcomes, and concrete nouns convert; metaphors and adjectives don't.
3. One page, one decision. Every section either earns the click on the primary CTA or removes a specific objection. Cut anything that does neither.
4. Objections are sequential, not parallel. Order sections to mirror the visitor's doubts: Is this for me? → Does it actually work? → Is it safe to try? → How do I start? Proof, demo, social, pricing, CTA — in that priority.
5. Show the product within the first viewport. A real screenshot, loom, or 6-second loop beats illustrations. Buyers click "see demo" because they don't trust your copy yet.
6. Social proof must be load-bearing, not decorative. Named customers, revenue/usage stats, and verbatim quotes with photos. Drop generic "5 stars" badges.
7. CTAs are commitments. Match the friction to the visitor's temperature: cold traffic → "See how it works", warm → "Start free", hot → "Get a demo". Never use "Submit".
8. Forms are tax. Each field cuts conversion ~7–11%. Ask only what's required to deliver value in the next step.
9. Speed is conversion. >2.5s LCP, >0.1 CLS, or layout shifts on CTA = you're leaking. Fix performance before copy.
10. Test the headline and hero image first. They account for the majority of conversion lift. Body copy and footer almost never move the metric.
