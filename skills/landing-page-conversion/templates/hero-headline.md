# Hero Headline Templates

Pick the pattern that matches your buyer's stage of awareness. Fill the brackets with concrete nouns and numbers — never adjectives.

---

## Pattern 1 — Outcome + Time Compression (best for solution-aware buyers)

**Headline:** Ship [specific deliverable] in [timeframe], not [previous timeframe].
**Subhead:** [Product name] is the [category] that [single concrete mechanism] so [target role] can [downstream business outcome].

> Ship a SOC 2 report in 6 weeks, not 6 months.
> Vanta is the compliance platform that automates 90% of evidence collection so security teams can pass audits without losing engineering quarters.

---

## Pattern 2 — Replace + Named Competitor (best for category-aware buyers)

**Headline:** The [adjective] alternative to [incumbent], built for [specific persona].
**Subhead:** Get [top 2 features of incumbent] without [incumbent's biggest pain]. Free for [usage tier].

> The fast alternative to Jira, built for product teams that actually ship.
> Get sprints, roadmaps, and reporting without the configuration tax. Free for teams under 10.

---

## Pattern 3 — Problem-Aware (best for cold traffic, content-led)

**Headline:** [Specific painful task] is broken. [Product] fixes the [root cause], not the symptom.
**Subhead:** For [target role] at [company stage]. Set up in [time]. Works with [top 2 tools they already use].

> Customer support is broken. Plain fixes the inbox, not the ticket count.
> For support leads at Series A–C SaaS. Set up in 20 minutes. Works with Stripe and Segment out of the box.

---

## Pattern 4 — Identity / Status (best for prosumer & creator tools)

**Headline:** For [specific identity] who [aspirational verb] [specific noun].
**Subhead:** [Product] gives you [the 2-3 capabilities a pro in this identity actually needs], without [the thing amateurs settle for].

> For indie founders who ship on weekends.
> Pieter gives you a deploy pipeline, error tracking, and revenue dashboards, without the AWS console.

---

## Bad pattern checklist — reject the headline if it:

- Uses the words: revolutionary, powerful, seamless, intuitive, robust, unleash, supercharge, next-gen
- Doesn't name the user
- Doesn't promise an outcome
- Could describe 3 competitors equally well
- Is longer than 12 words
