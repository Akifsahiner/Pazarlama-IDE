# Playbook: Landing Page With an Existing Email List

You have a warm channel. The job of the page changes: it must convert *known* visitors fast and segment *unknown* visitors cleanly.

- **Build a list-specific landing page**, not a homepage link. Match the subject line headline word-for-word ("message match"). This single change typically lifts paid traffic conversion 20–40% and works the same for email.
- **Pre-fill what you know.** If they clicked from a UTM tagged email, skip the email field on the signup form. Use a magic-link or one-click flow.
- **Add a "you, specifically" line** under the hero: "Because you opened our last 3 emails on pricing experiments, here's the tool." Personalization at the page level dramatically reduces bounce.
- **Run two CTAs in priority order**: primary = "Start the 7-day trial" (conversion), secondary = "Read the launch post" (nurture for not-yet-ready readers — keep them in the funnel).
- **Use your own list as proof.** "Joining 4,300 founders who get our weekly teardown" is real social proof you've already earned. Show subscriber count if >1,000.
- **Segment on the thank-you page**, not in the form. Single-question post-signup: "What brought you here today?" → routes to onboarding email sequence. This keeps conversion friction low and personalization high.
- **A/B test the headline against your best-performing email subject line.** Subject lines that win in inbox usually win on the page too — but verify, don't assume.

Watch: open→click→signup ratio per send; if click→signup <15%, the page is the bottleneck, not the email.
