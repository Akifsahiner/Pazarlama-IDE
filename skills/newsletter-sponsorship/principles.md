## Principles

1. **Newsletter sponsorship is native editorial, not display ads.** Copy must read like the curator recommending a tool — ≤120 words, first-person optional, one concrete outcome. Banner-speak ("revolutionary platform") gets skipped in 2 seconds.
2. **ICP overlap beats list size.** A 40k React newsletter with 70% frontend devs beats a 200k "tech news" digest with 10% fit. Score overlap before opening rate envy.
3. **Request media kit + 3 recent sponsor blocks** before any wire. You need: open rate (last 90d), click rate on sponsor slots, audience job titles, slot price, lead time, and whether they allow A/B copy.
4. **UTM before booking.** Every sponsor URL must carry `utm_source`, `utm_medium=newsletter_sponsor`, `utm_campaign`, `utm_content` (hook variant). No UTM = no kill rules — you cannot optimize blind.
5. **14-day minimum lead time** for quality newsletters (Bytes, React Status, TLDR Dev). Rush slots cost 2× and land in holiday issues with dead engagement.
6. **One newsletter per week max** when testing. Stacking 4 sponsors same week splits attribution and burns budget before you learn which list converts.
7. **A/B the hook, not the product.** Test opening line ("Ship faster CI" vs "Catch SQL typos pre-prod") — keep CTA and URL stable so UTM content param isolates the winner.
8. **7-day CPA kill rule is non-negotiable.** Define max acceptable CPA (e.g. $80 signup, $400 activated trial) *before* spend. Day 7: if CPA > ceiling with ≥200 clicks, pause that newsletter — do not "give it another week."
9. **CTR floor: 0.8% on sponsor block** (clicks / delivered opens). Below 0.5% after 2 sends = creative or audience mismatch — rewrite copy before rebooking.
10. **Max 2 consecutive slots** in the same newsletter. Readers fatigue; third week in a row trains ignore. Rotate lists or skip an issue between repeats.
11. **Teardown on D+7** per sponsor: clicks, signups, CPA, hook winner, qualitative inbox replies. This doc drives the next shortlist — not gut feel.
12. **Honest ceiling:** No landing page, no analytics, or B2C-only ICP → newsletter sponsorship is wasted spend; use `no-audience` runway to ship LP + UTM first.
