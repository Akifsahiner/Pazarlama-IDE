## Principles

1. **Nurture ≠ outbound.** These emails go to people who opted in or joined a waitlist. Cold prospecting belongs in `outreach-drafting` — different tone, different metrics (reply rate vs open/click/activate).
2. **Waitlist ≠ full sequence.** `waitlist-hype-engine` owns referral loop + single CTA capture. This skill owns **what happens after capture** through launch and onboarding.
3. **Segment before send.** Minimum segments: *engaged* (opened 2 of last 5), *warm* (opened 1), *dormant* (0 opens in 90d). Launch waves hit engaged first — never blast dormant at H0.
4. **Pre-launch drip is a story arc** — problem → build log → sneak peek → launch invite. Not "5% off every 3 days."
5. **Subject line A/B on teaser only** — test Day T-7 email (not launch day). Winner subject feeds wave 2; don't A/B on H0 (too noisy).
6. **Near-miss nudge is specific** — "You're 1 invite from early access" or "You opened our beta teaser but didn't activate — here's the link." Generic "last chance" fails.
7. **Three launch waves sync with PH/community** — Wave 1 engaged ~H+6 PT (after organic seed); Wave 2 general ~H+9; Wave 3 reframe ~H+12. Same clock as `ph_launch` when PH is primary.
8. **One CTA per email.** Launch email CTA = single URL (PH, signup, or demo). No menu of 4 links.
9. **Post-launch onboarding is 3 emails** — D+1 activation checklist, D+3 habit nudge (one feature), D+7 feedback ask. Stop there unless product-led lifecycle continues.
10. **Measure opens/clicks honestly** — if no ESP connector, log manual exports. No fabricated open rates.
11. **Unsubscribe is one click** — list-Unsubscribe header, visible footer link. Complaint rate >0.1% → pause waves.
12. **Honest ceiling:** List <200 or <15% identified contacts → expect 20–40% open on engaged segment; do not promise 60% launch blast opens.
