# Anti-Patterns

- **Don't:** Pitch without a news hook or proof metric. **Why:** Editors delete "startup launches product" in 3 seconds. **Cost:** Burned reporter contacts; reputation as noise.
- **Don't:** Send the same email to 50 reporters with `{FirstName}` mail-merge. **Why:** Beat reporters recognize templates instantly; Gmail threads get shared internally as examples of what not to do. **Cost:** Domain flagged; future pitches ignored.
- **Don't:** Miss or fuzzy the embargo datetime ("Tuesday morning"). **Why:** Embargo exists so outlets publish simultaneously; early leaks anger partners and violate trust. **Cost:** Blacklisted by desks; coordinated launch ruined.
- **Don't:** Leak the story on founder LinkedIn/X before embargo lifts. **Why:** Reporters who held the story lose the scoop incentive; some kill the piece. **Cost:** 0 pickups after weeks of prep.
- **Don't:** Fabricate customer logos or "enterprise clients" without permission. **Why:** Fact-checkers call customers; retractions damage credibility permanently. **Cost:** Legal exposure + public correction.
- **Don't:** Pay for pay-for-play "articles" disguised as journalism. **Why:** Readers and real reporters recognize sponsored fluff; Google may label it; it doesn't build real press relationships. **Cost:** Money spent, zero SEO/trust, possible FTC issues.
- **Don't:** Attach 25MB of uncompressed PNGs instead of a press kit PDF. **Why:** Large attachments bounce; reporters won't download. **Cost:** Pitch never opened.
- **Don't:** Write a founder quote longer than 60 words or full of jargon. **Why:** Editors truncate or replace with their own framing — often less favorable. **Cost:** Message drift in the story that ships.
- **Don't:** Pitch vertical/trade reporters with a consumer-app angle (or vice versa). **Why:** Mismatch signals you didn't read their beat. **Cost:** Instant delete; harder to pitch that desk again.
- **Don't:** Follow up more than once without a reply. **Why:** Persistence without value reads as harassment; reporters block senders. **Cost:** Lost access to the entire outlet.
- **Don't:** Use press release wire services (PR Newswire blast) as your primary strategy. **Why:** Wires are SEO noise for startups; tier-1 desks ignore them. **Cost:** $500–2k spent; zero human pickup.
- **Don't:** Schedule press for a launch with <7 days runway and no kit ready. **Why:** Reporters need 5–14 days lead time for non-breaking news. **Cost:** "Too late" replies; scrambled assets; missed embargo coordination.
- **Don't:** Promise reporters exclusives to multiple outlets simultaneously. **Why:** First to publish wins; others retract and warn peers. **Cost:** Industry reputation destroyed.
- **Don't:** Ignore a reporter's fact-check call during launch window. **Why:** Missing a 20-minute callback means the story runs with wrong pricing, wrong metric, or without your quote. **Cost:** Permanent incorrect article; no correction guarantee.
- **Don't:** Treat press pickups as the launch KPI when ICP doesn't read those outlets. **Why:** Vanity coverage in the wrong vertical drives zero signups. **Cost:** Team celebrates; pipeline empty.
