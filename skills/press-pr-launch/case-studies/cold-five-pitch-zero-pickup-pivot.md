# Case study: Cold 5-pitch press → 0 pickups, teardown saved launch (anonymous)

## Context

B2B workflow tool, ~400 waitlist, no funding, no reporter relationships. Founder wanted "launch week TechCrunch." Product ready; launch in 12 days. Proof: 8 design partners with one aggregate metric ("saved ~6h/week") but no named logo permission yet.

## Tactic stack applied

1. `pr_honest_coverage_ceiling` — team aligned on 0–1 cold ceiling before spend
2. `pr_press_kit_pdf` — 6-page kit with anonymous "mid-market ops teams" proof (no logos)
3. `pr_reporter_shortlist_5` — 3 trade SaaS reporters + 2 tier-2 tech; each beat-scored
4. `pr_pitch_personalized_hook` — 5 unique emails citing recent articles
5. `pr_embargo_datetime_lock` — Tuesday 9:00 UTC lift; no leaks
6. `pr_follow_up_sla_24h` — 2 reporters asked clarifying questions; answered same day
7. `pr_launch_monitoring_6h` — zero stories at H+6
8. `pr_kill_no_pickup_48h` — at H+48, pivoted to founder teardown + waitlist email

## Outcome

- **Press pickups:** 0
- **Pitch reply rate:** 2/5 (40%) — one "not a fit," one "maybe post-launch case study"
- **Teardown + email (owned media):** ~1.8k sessions, ~140 signups in 72h
- **Lesson captured:** missing named customer permission weakened hook; trade reporters wanted a logo

## Lesson

Zero pickups after honest cold outreach is **normal**, not a crisis — if you execute kill/pivot and owned media. Do not escalate to paid placement or 50-reporter blasts. Next cycle: secure one logo permission before pitching; one trade pickup likely beats ten cold tier-2 attempts.
