# Case study: DevTool with warm trade intro → 2 pickups + demo spike (anonymous)

## Context

Developer observability tool, seed-funded ($4.2M public), 14 days to launch. Assets: press kit PDF, 5 screenshots, founder quote approved, one **warm intro** to trade devtools reporter via angel investor. Three named design partners with written logo permission. ICP: platform engineers at 50–500 person SaaS.

## Tactic stack applied

1. `pr_embargo_datetime_lock` — Thursday 14:00 UTC; intro reporter confirmed
2. `pr_press_kit_pdf` — 7 pages including before/after latency metric with methodology footnote
3. `pr_customer_proof_permission` — 3 logo permissions archived
4. `pr_reporter_shortlist_5` — intro reporter + 4 others (2 trade, 2 tier-2); intro pitched T-10
5. `pr_pitch_personalized_hook` — cold 4 referenced recent incident/outage coverage angles
6. `pr_founder_quote_approved` — 52-word quote on observability debt; used verbatim in trade story
7. `pr_screenshot_asset_kit` — dark-mode dashboard + trace waterfall GIF still
8. `pr_launch_monitoring_6h` — trade story live at H+12; tier-2 syndication at H+22
9. `pr_follow_up_sla_24h` — fact-check call returned in 18 minutes; pricing corrected pre-publish
10. Email amplify post-lift (from `with-email-list`) — 6k list, 2 waves citing trade URL

## Outcome

- **Press pickups:** 2 (1 trade primary, 1 tier-2 syndication)
- **Press sessions (72h):** ~4.2k (`utm_source=press`)
- **Signups (7d):** ~380; ~18% from press referrer
- **Demos (14d):** 31 attributable; 4 closed in 45 days
- **Founder quote:** verbatim in trade piece

## Lesson

One warm trade intro + funding + permissioned logos beat twenty cold tier-1 pitches. Aggressive war room intensity (kit, embargo, SLA) protects the pickups you earn — especially the fact-check call that fixed seat pricing before print. Honest ceiling for this profile was 1–3, not "TechCrunch or bust."
