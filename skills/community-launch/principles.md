## Principles

1. **Show HN is not a press release.** Title format: `Show HN: [Product] – [one concrete outcome]`. The first comment is the maker story — problem, build, ask for feedback. Link to live demo in comment, not buried in title tricks.
2. **Tuesday–Thursday 8–10am PT** is the HN sweet spot for US tech readers. Weekend posts die. Don't launch the same hour as major tech news (Apple event, etc.).
3. **One community per 48 hours** unless you're replying in-thread. Cross-posting HN + Reddit + IH same day reads as spam and burns all three channels.
4. **Demo must work.** HN will click through in 30 seconds. Broken signup, 404, or "coming soon" kills the thread permanently — no second chance same week.
5. **Comments are the product.** On HN, thoughtful replies in the first 2 hours signal quality to moderators and readers. Block 4 hours for founder replies.
6. **Reddit is rules-first.** Each subreddit has self-promo rules. Lurk 2 weeks, contribute value, then post a story — never naked link drops.
7. **Indie Hackers rewards milestones.** "I hit $X MRR" or "Launching today" with build log beats product brochure. Include numbers when honest.
8. **Dev.to is SEO compounding.** Technical deep-dive + canonical link to your docs; not a duplicate landing page pitch.
9. **Front page ≠ revenue.** Measure signups from `news.ycombinator.com` referrer and 7-day retention — not upvote count alone.
10. **Teardown on D+1** with sessions, signups, and lessons — this post often outperforms the launch for long-tail distribution.
11. **Open source gets GitHub star CTA** in README before HN — not "star us pls" in title.
12. **Honest ceiling:** Cold account, no demo, or B2B enterprise-only ICP → expect modest traffic; use `no-audience` runway first.
