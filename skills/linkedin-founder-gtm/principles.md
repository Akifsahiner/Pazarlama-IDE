## Principles

1. LinkedIn rewards *founder authenticity*, not brand accounts. The company page gets 5–10× less reach than a founder posting the same content from a personal profile with 500+ relevant connections.
2. The headline is your billboard — 220 characters visible in feed previews. Formula: `[Role] helping [ICP] [outcome] | [Proof point] | [CTA or hook]`. Example: "Founder @ Acme — helping ops teams cut onboarding from 14 days to 2 | 40+ SaaS teams | DM 'demo'".
3. Content that teaches beats content that sells. The 70/20/10 split: 70% frameworks and lessons from building, 20% customer stories and proof, 10% direct product mentions. Overselling triggers unfollows.
4. The first line is the hook — it appears before "...see more." Use a contrarian claim, specific number, or open loop. "We lost $40k because of one Slack setting" beats "Excited to announce our new feature."
5. Comments are distribution. Spend 30 minutes daily commenting substantively on 5–10 posts from ICP leaders, investors, and peer founders. Thoughtful comments (3+ sentences) drive profile visits more than posting alone when starting out.
6. Document the journey, don't perform success. Build logs, mistakes, metrics (even small ones), and "what we'd do differently" posts outperform polished launch announcements for early-stage founders.
7. DM outbound works when it's *research*, not pitch. "Saw your post on [topic] — we're solving [related pain]. Would a 10-min call to compare notes be useful?" converts 3–5× better than "Check out our product."
8. Carousels and document posts get 2–3× engagement vs. text-only for how-to content. Use 8–10 slides: hook slide, problem, framework steps, proof slide, CTA slide. Export from Figma or Canva at 1080×1350.
9. Launch moments on LinkedIn are *threads*, not single posts. Day 0: problem story. Day +1: customer quote + screenshot. Day +3: metric or benchmark. Day +5: founder lesson. Sustained drumbeat beats one viral post.
10. Measure ICP engagement, not vanity likes. Track comments from target titles (VP Ops, Head of Marketing, etc.) and DM conversations started. 50 likes from random consultants < 5 comments from buyers.
