# Anti-Patterns

- **Don't:** Post from the company page as your primary GTM channel. **Why:** Algorithm deprioritizes brand pages. Founder personal profile is the distribution vehicle; company page is for credibility only.
- **Don't:** Use engagement pods or "comment for comment" groups. **Why:** LinkedIn detects artificial engagement patterns. Reach gets throttled; account can be restricted.
- **Don't:** Lead posts with "Excited to announce" or "Thrilled to share." **Why:** Pattern-matched as corporate spam. Start with the problem, metric, or story — announcement comes in paragraph 3.
- **Don't:** Connect-and-pitch in the same message. **Why:** Instant ignore or report. Connect with a blank or contextual note; pitch only after they've engaged with your content or replied.
- **Don't:** Post daily product screenshots with no narrative. **Why:** Reads as ads. Wrap every product mention in a customer outcome or build lesson.
- **Don't:** Ignore DMs for 48+ hours. **Why:** LinkedIn DM response time correlates with conversion. Reply within 4 hours during business hours; use saved replies for common questions.
- **Don't:** Copy viral LinkedIn templates verbatim ("Agree? 👇"). **Why:** Audience fatigue is real. Adapt the structure, not the words — authenticity beats formula.

- **Don't:** Ship generic advice untied to this product profile. **Why:** Founders already have ChatGPT. **Cost:** Trust and retention.

- **Don't:** Recommend five channels at once. **Why:** Dilutes learning. **Cost:** No clear kill signal.

- **Don't:** Invent metrics or connector data. **Why:** Honest measurement is a product promise. **Cost:** Credibility collapse.

- **Don't:** Skip acceptance criteria on tasks. **Why:** Ambiguous done. **Cost:** Endless 'almost shipped'.

- **Don't:** Hide tradeoffs. **Why:** Senior operators state ceilings. **Cost:** Overpromise churn.
