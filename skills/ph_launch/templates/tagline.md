# Product Hunt Tagline Templates

**Constraints:** ≤60 characters. Sentence case. Ends without a period. No emojis (PH strips them, and they look like 2017). The tagline appears on the front page next to the product name — it must tell a stranger what the product does in one glance.

---

## Pattern 1 — "X for Y"

The most successful PH pattern of the last decade because it's instantly mappable.

- `[Recognizable tool] for [new audience or use case]`

Examples (copy the form, not the words):
- Linear for marketing teams
- Figma for legal contracts
- Notion for engineering specs
- Cursor for SQL analysts
- Stripe for B2B physical goods

**When to use:** Your product borrows a known mental model. Avoid if you'd be misleading.

---

## Pattern 2 — Outcome verb + concrete object

Works when no obvious "X for Y" exists. Lead with a verb that promises an action the user wants to take.

- `[Verb] [specific object] in [timeframe / unit]`

Examples:
- Ship a SOC 2 report in 6 weeks
- Generate landing page copy from a Figma file
- Turn meeting recordings into shipped docs
- Cut payroll runtime from 4 hours to 9 minutes
- Run accessibility audits on every PR

---

## Pattern 3 — Replace pattern (use sparingly)

Only when the incumbent is universally disliked or genuinely outdated. Naming names is bold; do not do it cheaply.

- `The [adjective] [category] without [pain of incumbent]`
- `An open-source [incumbent]`

Examples:
- The fast Jira, built for product teams
- An open-source Datadog
- Notion without the lag
- Postgres for AI search

---

## Pattern 4 — Identity / "for X who Y"

Best for prosumer + creator tools where identity matters more than feature parity.

- `For [identity] who [aspirational verb] [specific noun]`

Examples:
- For indie founders who ship on weekends
- For PMs tired of writing status updates
- For analysts who live in BigQuery

---

## Pattern 5 — Demoable claim

A specific, falsifiable claim that the demo proves.

- `[Claim with a number] [for a named audience]`

Examples:
- 90% less prompt engineering for RAG apps
- 5-minute landing pages that actually convert
- Postgres → production in one command

---

## Reject the tagline if it:

- Contains "AI-powered", "next-gen", "revolutionary", "seamless", "intuitive", "platform", "solution"
- Is longer than 60 characters (it WILL be truncated on mobile)
- Could describe 3 competitors
- Doesn't name the user or the outcome
- Uses jargon a non-customer wouldn't understand
- Ends in a period or exclamation mark

---

## Final test

Read it aloud to someone who doesn't know your product. If their first question is "what does it do?" — rewrite it.
