## Principles

1. PH is a *24-hour traffic flash*, not a launch in itself. Plan everything around the curve: 12:01am PT submission, velocity wave for 6 hours, sustaining engagement for 18 more, then it's gone. Build the rest of your launch around that window.
2. Top 5 of the day is achievable on merit. Top 1 almost always requires a pre-built community or a hunter with reach. Aim for what you can earn — overreaching invites upvote-ring behavior and bans.
3. The tagline is 60 characters. It's the entire first impression. It must say what the product *does*, not what it *is*. "Spell-check for SQL" beats "AI-powered query intelligence."
4. The gallery sells the product, not the description. Slide 1 = the headline + product shot. Slide 2 = the core workflow in motion. Slides 3–6 = features users actually care about. Last slide = social proof.
5. The maker's first comment is the second most-read text on the page after the tagline. It should tell a story — why you built it, who it's for, what you want feedback on. Not a brochure.
6. Real comments compound. Hunters and curators reward threads with substantive conversation, not just upvotes. Every comment deserves a real reply within 30 minutes during the launch window.
7. Never ask for upvotes. Ask for honest feedback or for people to share their own pain. PH explicitly demotes upvote-ring patterns; the language model on the platform detects them.
8. Hunter ≠ marketing partner. A famous hunter gives you a launch-morning push, not sustained velocity. Most #1 products of the day are self-hunted by makers with engaged audiences.
9. Tuesday and Wednesday are the highest-traffic days. Avoid Mondays, Fridays, US holidays, and the week of any major industry conference.
10. PH success and revenue success are different KPIs. Measure both — and accept the rank is mostly a marketing artifact. The pipeline from PH visitors to retained users is the real win.
