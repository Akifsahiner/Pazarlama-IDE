# Anti-Patterns

- **Don't:** Ask for upvotes in DMs, tweets, or emails. **Why:** PH's algorithm detects upvote-ring language ("upvote us!", "give us a vote") and demotes the product. Ask for honest comments or feedback instead.
- **Don't:** Launch on a Monday, Friday, weekend, or US holiday. **Why:** Mondays = inbox triage, Fridays = engagement drops by noon, weekends = curators are offline. Tuesday/Wednesday are the standard for a reason.
- **Don't:** Submit at any time other than 12:01am Pacific. **Why:** PH's day starts at 12:01am PT. Submitting at 9am PT loses 9 hours of voting time and almost guarantees you finish off the top.
- **Don't:** Pay for a hunter or buy upvotes. **Why:** PH bans products and accounts caught doing this. The platform actively monitors. Recovery is near-impossible.
- **Don't:** Use a tagline with "AI-powered" or "Revolutionary." **Why:** The PH audience pattern-matches these to low-effort launches and scrolls past.
- **Don't:** Make the gallery slides mockups or illustrations. **Why:** PH viewers expect real product. Polished illustrations read as "vaporware" — actual screenshots, even ugly ones, convert better.
- **Don't:** Use a stock-photo-of-people thumbnail. **Why:** Looks generic among the 30+ launches that day. Use a clean product shot or a single bold word on color.
- **Don't:** Pre-announce the exact PH URL more than 6 hours before launch. **Why:** PH penalizes early traffic spikes that look manufactured.
- **Don't:** Schedule meetings, code, or shipping on launch day. **Why:** Founder presence in comments for the first 6 hours drives 30–50% of engagement. Block the entire day.
- **Don't:** Ignore non-English comments. **Why:** A huge chunk of PH's audience is non-US. Reply in their language (DeepL is fine). Curators notice.
- **Don't:** Engage with troll comments emotionally. **Why:** Public defensiveness is the fastest way to torch a launch. Reply once, factually; move on.
- **Don't:** Treat the launch as the campaign. **Why:** PH is one channel — 24 hours of traffic. The compounding wins come from the recap post, the followers gained, the inbound DMs, and the warm list for v2.
- **Don't:** Write a maker comment that reads like a press release. **Why:** PH readers can detect "marketing tone" immediately. Write in first person, in your real voice.
- **Don't:** Skip the "upcoming" page pre-launch. **Why:** It builds a subscriber list of 100–500 people who get auto-notified when you launch. Free velocity at 12:01am.
- **Don't:** Bundle multiple products in one launch. **Why:** PH wants one product per launch. Multi-product launches confuse curators and dilute the upvote signal.
