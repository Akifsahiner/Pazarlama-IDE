# Playbook: PH Launch With No Audience

Most "I launched and got 12 upvotes" stories come from launching cold. Don't. Build the runway first, then schedule the launch.

- **Don't launch in week 1.** Spend 21–30 days building the 100-supporter pool before scheduling. A cold launch on PH is functionally invisible — you'll hit page 4 and get no traffic.
- **Become a real PH citizen first.** For 14 days: upvote 3 products/day, leave 1 substantive comment, follow 20 makers in your category. The platform notices contribution patterns; they affect curation.
- **Recruit 30 design partners outside of PH** (X DMs, IndieHackers replies, Slack/Discord communities, cold emails to ICP). Onboard them. Get 5 honest quotes.
- **Find your hunter.** Browse the Hunters Leaderboard, find someone active in your category with 2k+ followers and recent successful hunts. DM them 14 days out with a clear ask: "Would you hunt my product on [date]? Here's a 60-second demo." Don't pay; quality hunters won't accept money.
- **Pre-list on the upcoming page** 7 days out. This builds a "notify me" list of subscribers who'll see a launch notification automatically. Even 200 of these matter.
- **The night before**: line up 10 people who promise to comment (not upvote — *comment*) in the first hour with real questions. Real first-hour engagement is what gets you noticed by curators.
- **Launch at 12:01am PT Tuesday or Wednesday.** Submit, then sleep 4 hours. Wake at 5am PT for the European wake-up wave. Respond to every comment until 8pm PT.
- **The day after, write the launch teardown post** with screenshots, numbers, and lessons. This is your real distribution; it travels further than the PH page itself.

Realistic outcome cold: ranks #15–25, 800–1,500 page visits, 60–200 signups, ~30 net new followers.
