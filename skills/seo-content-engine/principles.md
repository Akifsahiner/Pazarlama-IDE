## Principles

1. **Intent before volume.** Classify keywords into informational (how-to), commercial (alternatives, vs), and transactional (pricing, signup). Dev tools win on commercial comparison intent first — not broad "AI marketing" head terms.
2. **One primary cluster per sprint.** Ship 3–5 pages in one semantic cluster (e.g. "[Competitor] alternatives") before opening a second cluster. Thin scatter across 20 topics ranks nothing.
3. **Comparison pages need honest tables.** Name real competitors, cite verifiable differences (pricing model, self-host, API), and disclose your bias. Google and buyers punish generic "best 10 tools" listicles.
4. **Programmatic ≠ duplicate.** Template-driven pages still need unique intro paragraph, screenshot, and ICP-specific use case per URL. Same skeleton + swapped noun = thin content risk.
5. **Internal links are architecture.** Every docs page should link to one commercial page and one signup path; every blog post links to docs depth + product. Orphan pages do not compound.
6. **Docs CTAs are contextual.** End-of-article CTA after value delivered — not popover on first scroll. Developer trust drops fast on aggressive interstitials.
7. **Indexation is the first KPI.** If GSC shows "Discovered — not indexed," fix canonicals, sitemap, and internal links before writing more copy.
8. **Title tag formula for dev tools:** `[Product] vs [Competitor]: [one differentiator]` or `[Competitor] alternatives for [ICP job]`. ≤60 chars where possible.
9. **Schema where it helps.** `SoftwareApplication`, `FAQPage` on comparison pages — not spammy `AggregateRating` without real reviews.
10. **Measure organic in GSC + product analytics.** Sessions from `/organic` or `google / organic` referrer → signup rate. No fabricated GA4 rows when connector absent.
11. **Refresh cadence D+30.** Pages with impressions >1000 and CTR <2% get title/meta refresh; pages with 0 impressions after 60d get merged or noindexed.
12. **Honest ceiling:** New domain, no backlinks, no brand search → expect 3–6 months to meaningful organic; state that when user asks for "SEO in 2 weeks."
