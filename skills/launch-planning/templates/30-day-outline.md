# 30-Day Launch Outline (Copy & Adapt)

Replace `[X]` with your product, ICP, and channels. Each day lists the **single most important task** — not everything you might do.

---

## Week 1 — Foundation (Days 1–7)

**Goal: Decide what you're launching, to whom, and on which day.**

- **Day 1 (Mon):** Write the launch thesis in one sentence. "On [date], we launch [product] to [ICP] to hit [metric]."
- **Day 2 (Tue):** Lock the launch date. Tuesday or Wednesday of week 5. Avoid Mondays (people clearing inboxes), Fridays (low engagement), and holiday-adjacent weeks.
- **Day 3 (Wed):** Write a 1-pager: pain → product → 3 proof points → CTA. This becomes the spine of every asset.
- **Day 4 (Thu):** List every owned channel with reach numbers. List, X, LinkedIn, Discord/Slack, partners, customer base. Total = realistic launch-week reach.
- **Day 5 (Fri):** Set the primary KPI + 3 secondary KPIs. Write them on the team doc.
- **Day 6–7 (Sat/Sun):** Founder rest. Burnout in week 1 kills launches in week 5.

---

## Week 2 — Asset Build (Days 8–14)

**Goal: Every asset shippable by end of week. No new asset work after Day 14.**

- **Day 8:** 90-second demo video (Loom is fine; polish ≠ conversion).
- **Day 9:** Rebuild the landing page hero + CTA. See `landing-page-conversion` skill.
- **Day 10:** Draft launch tweet thread + LinkedIn post + PH tagline + first comment. See `ph_launch/templates`.
- **Day 11:** Email sequence: T-7, T-2, T-0 (3am), T+1 (thank-you), T+5 (recap).
- **Day 12:** 3 customer quotes with name + title + headshot + metric. If you don't have customers, recruit 3 design partners *today*.
- **Day 13:** Press list (5 reporters, 5 newsletters, 5 podcasts). Personal angle for each.
- **Day 14:** Internal review with 3 outside-the-team eyes. Cut anything they don't understand in 60 seconds.

---

## Week 3 — Warm-up (Days 15–21)

**Goal: Build proof and pre-commitments. Avoid surprise on launch day.**

- **Day 15:** First "build in public" post teasing the launch.
- **Day 16:** Email the list: "Launching in 2 weeks — here's a sneak peek." Measure open rate.
- **Day 17:** Reach out to your top 30 supporters individually. Personal DMs, not blasts.
- **Day 18:** Brief press under embargo if applicable.
- **Day 19:** Soft-pitch to 3 niche communities ("we'd love your feedback before launch").
- **Day 20:** Pre-record any podcast appearances to release launch week.
- **Day 21:** Dry-run: have a non-team member follow the entire signup → activation flow on mobile. Fix what breaks.

---

## Week 4 — Final Mile (Days 22–28)

**Goal: Calendar locked, team aligned, contingencies ready.**

- **Day 22:** Send T-7 email to full list.
- **Day 23:** Schedule all launch-week social posts (with hand-edited times, not bulk).
- **Day 24:** PH hunter confirmed + thumbnail/gallery uploaded as draft.
- **Day 25:** Brief design partners with the exact ask + the exact link + the exact time window for engagement.
- **Day 26:** Stress-test: server load, signup flow, payment, support inbox.
- **Day 27:** Write the "if we flop" plan and the "if we blow up" plan. Tell the team both.
- **Day 28:** Team alignment call. Roles for launch day: comms, support, social, ops, founder.

---

## Week 5 — Launch (Days 29–30+)

- **Day 29 (Launch Tue):** 12:01am PT — PH live. 6am ET — press embargo lifts, email + social go. Founder responds to every comment for the first 6 hours. No meetings. No code.
- **Day 30 (Wed):** Daily recap thread. Personal thank-you DMs to top supporters. Re-pitch press with day-1 metrics.

---

## Post-launch (Days 31–60)

- Week 6: Convert overflow. 1 customer story published per week.
- Week 7: Pitch the "what we learned launching" angle to newsletters & podcasts.
- Week 8: Plan v2 launch or feature drop. Launches compound when spaced 60–90 days apart.
