# Anti-Patterns

- **Don't:** Launch on a Monday or Friday. **Why:** Mondays = inbox triage; Fridays = engagement craters by noon. Tuesday and Wednesday have the highest sustained attention curves.
- **Don't:** Launch on PH the same week as a YC batch demo day or a major Apple/Google keynote. **Why:** You're competing for upvotes against well-funded crowds with full marketing teams.
- **Don't:** Coordinate a launch with no defined primary metric. **Why:** "Big launch" with no number means everyone optimizes for vibes; on day +5 nobody can say if it worked.
- **Don't:** Ask for PH upvotes directly. **Why:** PH algorithmically demotes products with upvote rings; the platform reads the language pattern. Ask for *comments* or *feedback*.
- **Don't:** Email-blast the entire list at 9am launch day. **Why:** ESPs throttle; PH velocity bunches in one hour then dies. Stagger sends to spread velocity over 6+ hours.
- **Don't:** Take meetings on launch day. **Why:** Founder presence in comments + DMs in the first 6 hours drives 30–50% of the engagement curve. It is not delegable.
- **Don't:** Schedule the launch and then add features in the final 7 days. **Why:** Bugs ship with launches. The launch goal is awareness + conversion, not feature completeness.
- **Don't:** Plan a "viral" video as the centerpiece. **Why:** Viral is not a strategy; it's an outcome. Plan for steady channel-by-channel detonation; treat virality as upside.
- **Don't:** Buy reviews, fake testimonials, or paid upvotes. **Why:** PH bans; communities catch on; trust torched. Recovery from a public credibility loss takes 12+ months.
- **Don't:** Have multiple "launches" in a 30-day window. **Why:** Audience fatigue. People will stop opening your emails. Bundle into one event; save the rest for 60-day cadence.
- **Don't:** Skip the dry-run on mobile signup flow. **Why:** ~60% of launch-day traffic is mobile. A broken iOS form means you spent 30 days for nothing.
- **Don't:** Use a generic "we're launching!" subject line. **Why:** Open rates collapse. Lead with the *thing* ("Cursor for spreadsheets is live"), not the *act* of launching.
- **Don't:** Define launch success as "we shipped." **Why:** Shipping is the input, not the outcome. The launch is what happens to demand, not what happens to git.
- **Don't:** Wait until launch day to pitch press. **Why:** Tier-1 reporters need 5–10 days lead time + embargo. Same-day pitches are background noise.
- **Don't:** Plan only the launch — not the day after. **Why:** The day-after recap post is often the second-largest traffic spike. If you didn't plan it, you'll miss it.
