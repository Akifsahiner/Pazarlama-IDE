# Playbook: B2B SaaS Launch

B2B launches optimize for *pipeline*, not vanity. Top of PH won't matter if the buyer isn't an indie hacker. Lead with the channels your ICP actually reads.

- **Day −30 to −21: Build the proof pack.** Three case studies (named logo, metric, quote, headshot), one ROI calculator, one analyst-quality benchmark report. These are the launch's load-bearing assets.
- **Day −21 to −14: Brief journalists under embargo.** Pitch 5 specific reporters at TechCrunch, The Information, Forbes, plus 3 vertical pubs (e.g. The Stack, Pragmatic Engineer, depending on ICP). Embargo lift = launch morning, 6am ET.
- **Day −14: Engage 5 enterprise design partners** to publish testimonial posts on LinkedIn on launch day. Their reach is your distribution. Send them pre-written copy + screenshot they can paste.
- **Day −7: Pre-brief 3 industry analysts** (Forrester, Gartner, IDC, or vertical-specific). They won't write same-week but they'll quote you in the next category note.
- **Day −1: Sales team trained on launch-specific scripts.** Inbound demos will spike 5–10×; if SDRs aren't ready, qualified leads leak.
- **Launch Day, 6am ET: Press embargo lifts. Founder LinkedIn post + thread goes live. Email to list + customers + partners simultaneously.** PH is a *bonus channel*, not the spine. Aim Top 10; don't sacrifice ICP focus chasing #1.
- **Launch Day +1 to +5: Daily content drumbeat on LinkedIn**: customer story, founder note, benchmark chart, integration announce, hiring post. LinkedIn is the B2B distribution channel; treat it as PH.
- **Days +7 to +30: ABM-style outreach** to lookalikes of design partners. Use the launch posts as the "proof packet" in the cold sequence.

Realistic outcome: 50–200 qualified demos in week one, 8–25 closed deals in 60 days, 3–6 press mentions.
