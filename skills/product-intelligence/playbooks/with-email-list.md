# Playbook: Product Intelligence With an Existing Email List

You have subscribers — that's a weak signal of who *thought* they were your audience. Product intelligence now cross-checks repo evidence against list behavior and self-reported intent.

- **Pull list metadata before reading code.** Segment by: signup source (landing page, PH, content), signup date cohort, and any onboarding survey answers ("What brought you here?"). If 70%+ came from one landing headline, that headline is your strongest positioning signal.
- **Compare list persona to landing copy persona.** If emails signed up from "For Shopify operators" but README targets "enterprise data teams," you have two ICPs — document both and mark which has more volume.
- **Mine welcome-sequence replies for JTBD language.** Founders often get unprompted replies: "I've been looking for exactly this because…" Extract verbatim pain phrases — they beat hero copy for outreach and ads.
- **Use open/click patterns as channel-fit hints.** High open on "pricing experiments" emails + low open on "feature updates" → audience cares about ROI, not features. Feed that into positioning emphasis (outcome over capability).
- **Validate ICP tiers against subscriber domains.** Sample 50 work emails: group by company size (Clearbit/manual lookup), industry, and role if known. If 80% are solo founders but positioning says "enterprise," reposition or accept narrow ICP.
- **Cross-reference competitors from unsubscribe reasons and replies.** "I already use [X]" in replies is free competitive intel — add to wedge table with the specific switching trigger they implied.
- **Pricing signal from list engagement.** If you sent pricing teasers: click rate on "$29/mo" vs "$99/mo" subject lines hints at willingness-to-pay before `/pricing` exists.
- **Output dual profiles when needed.** Primary ICP = list-proven segment. Secondary ICP = repo-intended segment. Flag divergence explicitly — downstream skills pick primary unless user overrides.
- **Attach "list evidence" citations alongside file citations.** Example: "ICP role = Head of Growth (42% of last 200 signups, onboarding Q1)."

Track: ICP alignment score (list domain match vs stated ICP), claims validated by list behavior vs repo-only claims.

## Preconditions
- [ ] Marketing profile complete for product-intelligence
- [ ] Primary metric named before execution
- [ ] Honest aggression dial matches real assets (list, proof, team hours)

## Aggression dial

| Level | When | Focus |
|-------|------|-------|
| conservative | Thin assets / solo | One channel, learning metrics |
| standard | Warm channel or list | Best-practice cadence from narrative above |
| aggressive | Strong proof + distribution | Execute full tactic stack with kill rules |

## Timeline
- **T-14**: Instrument leading metric; lock ICP + offer
- **T-7**: Ship assets referenced in narrative
- **T-1**: Final checklist + supporter warm-up
- **H0**: Primary launch motion
- **D+1…D+7**: Measure, teardown, kill or scale

## Tactic stack
1. Name bottleneck with 3 profile citations
2. Ship one measurable experiment this week
3. Tie Plan Studio tasks to registry tactic ids
4. Cap parallel channels at 2
5. Predefine kill/pivot rule before spend or blast

## Orchestration
- One primary channel from narrative; ≤2 support channels
- Repo/browser/asset tasks split by execution_mode

## Realistic outcomes
- State honest bands — do not promise #1 PH or viral without assets
- Leading metric moves in 7–14d when playbook matches profile

## Kill / pivot rules
- Leading metric misses after agreed sample → stop and rewrite hypothesis
- Never add channels when primary motion underperforms

## Ethics line
- No fake metrics, fake social proof, undisclosed ads, or ToS-breaking growth hacks
