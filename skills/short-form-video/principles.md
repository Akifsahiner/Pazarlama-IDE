## Principles

1. Short-form is a *hook-retention-CTA* machine, not a TV ad. First 1–3 seconds decide everything. Open with motion, a bold claim, or a visual pattern interrupt — never a logo splash or "Hey guys."
2. Retention curve is the algorithm. Platforms push videos where >50% of viewers watch past 3 seconds and >30% reach the midpoint. Script and edit for hold, not polish.
3. One idea per video. "How we cut onboarding time 80%" is one video. "Our 5 features" is five videos. Multi-topic clips get skipped at the first topic change.
4. Native format wins: 9:16 vertical, 15–45 seconds for discovery, captions burned in (85% watch muted). Repurposed horizontal demos with letterboxing underperform 3–5×.
5. Hook formulas that work: contrarian ("Stop doing X"), specific number ("We saved $12k in 10 minutes"), before/after split screen, founder face + direct address ("If you're a [ICP role], watch this").
6. CTA belongs at the end *and* in caption. Verbal CTA last 3 seconds: "Link in bio" or "Comment DEMO." Pin comment with URL. Bio link must match video promise — message mismatch kills conversion.
7. Post volume beats perfection early. 3–5 videos/week for 30 days builds algorithmic learning. Batch film 10 videos in one session; edit in CapCut or Descript. Iteration > production value.
8. Comment section is SEO. Reply to every comment in first hour; ask a question in the video to drive comments ("What's your biggest [pain]?"). High comment rate extends distribution.
9. Trending audio and formats are accelerants, not strategy. Jump on trends only when you can tie to ICP pain in 48 hours. Evergreen problem-solution content compounds; trends spike and die.
10. Measure hook rate (3-sec views / impressions) and completion rate separately. Low hook = rewrite opening. Low completion = tighten middle or shorten. Don't change both at once — isolate variables.
