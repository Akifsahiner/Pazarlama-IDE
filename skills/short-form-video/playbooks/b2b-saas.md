# Playbook: Short-Form Video for B2B SaaS

B2B on TikTok/Reels/Shorts works when you teach ICP pain, not pitch features. Founder face + screen demo + specific outcome is the winning format.

- **Day −14: Set up channels.** TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts — same handle. Bio: one-line value prop + link (Linktree or direct landing with UTM). Pin best-performing video when you have 3+.
- **Day −12: Batch-script 15 videos.** Templates: (1) "Stop doing [common mistake]" — 30 sec, (2) Before/after screen demo — 25 sec, (3) "POV: You're a [ICP role] and [pain happens]" — 20 sec, (4) One specific metric story — 35 sec, (5) Reply to comment/DM as video — 15 sec. Each script: Hook (0–3s) → Problem (3–12s) → Solution demo (12–25s) → CTA (25–30s).
- **Day −10: Film batch (2 hours).** Founder on camera for hooks; screen record product for demo sections. Phone vertical 1080×1920. Natural light, lapel mic optional. 15 raw clips minimum.
- **Day −7: Edit in CapCut/Descript.** Burn captions (large, high contrast). Cut every pause >0.5 sec. Add zoom on key UI moments. Export 9:16, 30fps. No intro card.
- **Day −5: Publish first 3 videos (Mon/Wed/Fri).** Stagger platforms same day. Caption formula: hook repeat + one sentence context + CTA + 3 hashtags (#saas #founder #[niche]). Reply to every comment in first 60 min.
- **Day −3: Analyze hook retention.** Platform analytics: 3-sec rate per video. Kill hooks <35%; reshoot openings for bottom 2. Winning hook pattern → script 5 more in that style.
- **Day 0: Launch-week video.** "We just launched [Product] — here's the 20-second version of what it does." Hook: specific outcome. CTA: "Link in bio — founding pricing ends Friday." Cross-post to LinkedIn as native video (separate upload, not link).
- **Days +1 to +7: 3 videos/week rhythm.** Mix formats from batch; introduce 2 "reply to comment" videos from real user questions. Track: views, hook rate, completion, bio clicks.
- **Days +8 to +14: Double down on winner.** Identify top 2 videos by completion rate. Create 5 variations: same hook structure, different examples. Series content ("Part 2") if episodic hook worked.
- **Days +15 to +30: UGC and customer clips.** Ask 3 customers for 15-sec Loom: "One thing [Product] changed for us." Edit with caption overlay + their quote. Social proof format often outperforms founder-only.

Realistic outcome: 12–20 videos published, 1–3 videos with 10k+ views, 50–65% hook retention on best performers, 8–25 signups attributed, 500–2k profile followers.

## Preconditions
- [ ] Marketing profile complete for short-form-video
- [ ] Primary metric named before execution
- [ ] Honest aggression dial matches real assets (list, proof, team hours)

## Aggression dial

| Level | When | Focus |
|-------|------|-------|
| conservative | Thin assets / solo | One channel, learning metrics |
| standard | Warm channel or list | Best-practice cadence from narrative above |
| aggressive | Strong proof + distribution | Execute full tactic stack with kill rules |

## Timeline
- **T-14**: Instrument leading metric; lock ICP + offer
- **T-7**: Ship assets referenced in narrative
- **T-1**: Final checklist + supporter warm-up
- **H0**: Primary launch motion
- **D+1…D+7**: Measure, teardown, kill or scale

## Tactic stack
1. Name bottleneck with 3 profile citations
2. Ship one measurable experiment this week
3. Tie Plan Studio tasks to registry tactic ids
4. Cap parallel channels at 2
5. Predefine kill/pivot rule before spend or blast

## Orchestration
- One primary channel from narrative; ≤2 support channels
- Repo/browser/asset tasks split by execution_mode

## Realistic outcomes
- State honest bands — do not promise #1 PH or viral without assets
- Leading metric moves in 7–14d when playbook matches profile

## Kill / pivot rules
- Leading metric misses after agreed sample → stop and rewrite hypothesis
- Never add channels when primary motion underperforms

## Ethics line
- No fake metrics, fake social proof, undisclosed ads, or ToS-breaking growth hacks
