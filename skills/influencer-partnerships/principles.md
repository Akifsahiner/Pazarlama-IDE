## Principles

1. Influencer partnerships are *distribution buys*, not logo placements. Evaluate creators on audience-ICP overlap and engagement quality, not follower count. A 15k-account niche operator outperforms a 500k lifestyle creator for B2B SaaS.
2. The brief is a creative constraint, not a script. Provide: one-line value prop, 3 talking points, do/don't list, disclosure requirements, and tracking link. Let the creator's voice carry the message — forced scripts get skipped or feel inauthentic.
3. Structure deals on performance where possible. Hybrid model: base fee (50–70% of quote) + CPA or rev-share on qualified signups. Aligns incentives; filters creators who believe in the product.
4. Micro-influencers (5k–50k) often deliver 3–5× better CPA than macro. They reply to DMs, have loyal audiences, and accept product-for-post or $200–800 deals. Test 5 micro before 1 macro.
5. Give creators *exclusive* angles. Early access, founder interview, custom discount code with their name, or co-branded asset. Exclusivity increases share motivation and audience trust.
6. Track every partnership with unique UTM + promo code. Attribute signups, trials, and revenue per creator. Kill underperformers after one campaign; double down on top quartile.
7. Long-term ambassador beats one-off sponsored post. 3-month retainers with monthly deliverables (1 post, 1 story, 1 mention) build familiarity. Audiences need 3–7 touchpoints before converting.
8. Podcast and newsletter integrations outperform feed posts for B2B. Host-read ads, guest spots, and newsletter sponsorships drive higher-intent traffic than Instagram Reels for enterprise-adjacent products.
9. Legal and disclosure are non-negotiable. FTC/ASA require clear #ad or "paid partnership" labels. Provide disclosure language in the brief; creators who skip it are liability.
10. Post-campaign debrief with every creator. What worked, what didn't, audience feedback. Top partners become case studies and referral sources for recruiting similar creators.
